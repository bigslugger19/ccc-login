How to Upload Images to Dropbox

Create a Dropbox Account

Go to www.Dropbox.com.

You’ll see two options: Login or Create an Account. Select Create an Account and enter you name, email, and a password.

Install Dropbox on Your Computer

There are two versions of Dropbox: the online version and the desktop version. For this project, you will need to install the Desktop version on your computer.

Why? Using the Dropbox application is really convenient and fast. In fact, using Dropbox on your computer is just like using any other folder on your hard drive, except the files you drag into your Dropbox folder automatically sync online and to any other computers or mobile devices linked to your account. The application runs in the background and automatically keeps your files in sync and backed up online. It's like having the same folder on all of your computers and mobile devices at the same time.

The desktop application is really convenient because you never need to manually upload or download files in your Dropbox folder. The application automatically watches your Dropbox folder and keeps your files in sync for you. Any time you save a change to a file and are connected to the internet, that change will sync everywhere you have Dropbox installed.

The desktop application even works when you go offline. The next time you're online, Dropbox will sync changes just where it left off.

Upload Images to Dropbox

Once you install the Desktop version, it creates a new folder on your computer called Dropbox. Your Dropbox folder works just like any other folder on your hard drive, except everything in your Dropbox folder automatically syncs to the web and to any other computer with Dropbox installed.

Move your image files into your Dropbox by dragging and dropping them into your Dropbox folder. The files in your Dropbox folder will automatically be synced online and to your other computers. NOTE: Before you upload your images, you must create a specific set of folders to organize them (see next section, How to Organize Your Files in Dropbox).

How to Organize Your Files in Dropbox

All CCC Projects must use the following folder structure so that the information is consistent between projects. Create your folders in Dropbox exactly as follows:

  1. Create a folder for your project called “CCC Camera Study Project.”
    📂 CCC Camera Study Project
  2. Within it, create four subfolders:
    📂 Uncatalogued Camera Study Area Photos
    This folder is the first stop for your images after you’ve downloaded them from your camera’s SD card. It contains all images that have not yet been “catalogued” or uploaded to the database file.
    📂 Archived Photos
    After you have uploaded your images to the database, they will be stored here.
    📂 Database Files
    As the name implies, this folder contains your main database file and all additional versions of it that you create.
    📂 Master Forms and Manuals
    This folder is a repository for important documents such as the CCC Handbook handbook, data forms, and pre-field checklist.
  3. Within the Uncatalogued Camera Study Area Photos folder, create a new folder for each Study Area that has the official location name for the property, such as Lory State Park.
    📂 Uncatalogued Camera Study Area Photos
    📂 Lory State Park
  4. Within your Study Area folder, create a new folder that further defines the study area. This is your Location ID folder. Choose a name that includes some reference to the immediate location of the image set. It could the name of a particular trail or describe a key landscape feature, such as Rocky Hill Trailhead, Ravine, Saddle, etc.
    📂 Uncatalogued Camera Study Area Photos
    📂 Lory State Park
    📂 Rocky Hill Trailhead
  5. Within your Location ID folder, create a folder named after the date the images were removed from the camera. This is your Camera Check Date folder.

    NOTE: You must use the following date format or the database will not be able to locate your images:

    mm.dd.yyyy

    Example: March 1, 2015 would be 03.01.2015

    📂 Uncatalogued Camera Study Area Photos
    📂 Lory State Park
    📂 Rocky Hill Trailhead
    📂 03.01.2015

    You are now finished creating the folder structure for the Uncatalogued Camera Study Area Photos.

  6. Return to your Archived Photos folder.
  7. Within your Archived Photos folder, create a folder called “Highlight Photos.” This is where you will save the best photos captured from your camera traps. To learn more, see page 29, “How to Highlight Photos”).
    📂 Archived Photos
    📂 Highlight Photos
  8. Within your Highlight Photos folder, create a Study Area folder like you did in Step 3.
    📂 Archived Photos
    📂 Highlight Photos
    📂 Lory State Park
  9. Within your Study Area folder, create a Location ID folder like you did in Step 4.
    📂 Archived Photos
    📂 Highlight Photos
    📂 Lory State Park
    📂 Rocky Hill Trailhead
  10. Finally, within your Location ID folder, create a folder indicating the year the images were taken.
    📂 Archived Photos
    📂 Highlight Photos
    📂 Lory State Park
    📂 Rocky Hill Trailhead
    📂 2015

The Importance of Backing Up Your Image Files

In addition to uploading your images to Dropbox, we strongly recommend backing up your images to an external hard drive. That way if someone accidentally deletes your Dropbox files or you are unable to access your account, your original images files will still be accessible.
